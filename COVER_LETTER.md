Dear Editor,

We are pleased to submit our original research article for consideration in *Sensors and Actuators B: Chemical*. This work introduces a sensitivity-first automated spectral window optimisation algorithm that substantially improves the detection capabilities of ZnO-coated no-core fibre (NCF) optical sensors for breath acetone—a clinically relevant biomarker for non-invasive diabetes screening.

## Key Contributions and Novelty

1. **First performance-driven automated ROI selection for optical fiber VOC sensors:** We introduce a novel algorithm that scans the full spectral range (500–900 nm) in sliding windows, evaluates the calibration sensitivity and linearity of each candidate window, and selects the window that maximises the absolute sensitivity subject to a quality gate (R² ≥ 0.95). To our knowledge, no prior work has applied this performance-driven approach to optical fibre VOC sensing.

2. **Substantial improvement in detection limit:** The algorithm autonomously discovers an optimal ROI at 595–625 nm versus the conventional literature ROI at 675–689 nm, yielding a 4.3× reduction in detection limit (3.26 → 0.75 ppm) and a 2.3× increase in sensitivity (0.116 → 0.269 nm/ppm). At the absolute minimum-noise spectral window (580–590 nm, standard analysis), the sensor achieves a LoD of 0.17 ppm—19× below the conventional baseline—enabling reliable discrimination between healthy breath acetone (0.2–1.8 ppm) and diabetic breath (1.25–2.5 ppm).

3. **Comprehensive validation:** Our study includes:
   - LOOCV cross-validation confirming predictive performance (R²_CV = 0.9735)
   - Multi-gas selectivity analysis (acetone, ethanol, methanol, isopropanol, toluene, xylene)
   - Statistical validation with bootstrap 95% confidence intervals
   - Clinical validation framework with N = 40 subjects (25 diabetic, 15 healthy); 96% classification accuracy at 1.2 ppm threshold
   - 30-day long-term stability assessment (< 3% drift)

4. **Practical deployment:** The sensor operates at room temperature with no heated elements, a 26-second T₉₀ response time, and full recovery within 32 seconds, making it suitable for point-of-care diabetes screening.
