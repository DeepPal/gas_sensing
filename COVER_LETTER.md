Dear Editor,

We are pleased to submit our original research article for consideration in *Sensors and Actuators B: Chemical*. This work presents a novel approach combining spectral feature engineering with deep learning to significantly enhance the detection capabilities of optical fiber sensors for breath acetone—a clinically relevant biomarker for diabetes monitoring.

## Key Contributions and Novelty

1. **First-of-its-kind application:** This is the first reported application of spectral feature engineering (first-derivative convolution) to optical fiber VOC sensors, representing a paradigm shift in how weak absorbers can be detected using optical sensing platforms.

2. **Significant performance improvement:** We demonstrate a 77% reduction in detection limit (from 3.26 ppm to 0.76 ppm), achieving clinically relevant sub-ppm detection capability that enables reliable discrimination between healthy individuals (0.2-1.8 ppm) and diabetic patients (1.25-2.5 ppm).

3. **Comprehensive validation:** Our study includes:
   - Multi-gas selectivity analysis (acetone, ethanol, methanol, isopropanol, toluene, xylene)
   - Statistical validation with paired t-tests and effect size analysis
   - Clinical validation framework with 96% classification accuracy
   - Long-term stability assessment (30 days)

4. **Practical deployment:** The sensor operates at room temperature, requires no heated elements, and provides rapid response (18 seconds), making it suitable for point-of-care diabetes screening applications.
